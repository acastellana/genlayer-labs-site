// 03 — synthetic market research

Millions of environments.
One question:
what does the agentic economy want?

Traditional market research asks people what they want. People don't know. They can't describe a product that doesn't exist yet. You get JIRA tickets for faster horses.

Phylogenesis doesn't ask. It runs the experiment. Generate millions of synthetic economic environments — each one a different market, a different set of agents, a different use case hypothesis. Let them compete. Measure what survives.

The survivors don't tell you what people say they want. They tell you what an economy selects for when the constraint is survival. That is a different and more reliable signal than any focus group.

Each synthetic environment is a query against the future. The compute cost to run it is the research budget. You are converting FLOPS into market intelligence.
